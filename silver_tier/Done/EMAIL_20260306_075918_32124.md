Hey there,



Freelancing sounds like freedom…



You get to work from home, set your own hours, and avoid having a

boss.



But in reality, it just means more stress packed into your day in

a different way.



Because instead of one manager, you’ve now got a bunch of clients

all asking for things at once.



You gotta deal with late-night messages, sudden changes, and

problems you didn’t even cause.



And somehow, you end up spending more time sending quotes and

chasing payments than actually building something cool…



But there’s a better option you probably haven’t heard of...



And wiith this approach, you’re not working for clients or

waiting for someone to hire you.



All you gotta do is build small projects using tools you already

like, then share the process publicly.



For example, you might create a basic chat app using Supabase and

post a short video walkthrough.



Or you could write a blog that explains how you built a dashboard

with Appsmith.



And you could also upload a GitHub repo with working code and

clear setup steps for something useful.



This kind of content helps other devs learn faster...



And that’s exactly what a lot of developer-focused companies are

looking for.



When another dev watches your demo or reads your guide, they’re

more likely to try the product themselves.



That’s far more effective for these companies than just running

ads.



It’s why companies like Appsmith, Supabase, and Clerk have

programs to support content like this.



Appsmith, for example, pays between $200 and $400 per tutorial or

video.



Others like Supabase and Clerk offer grants, sponsorships, or

co-marketing deals if your content shows real use cases.



You can apply directly through their DevRel or marketing pages,

or simply share your demo online and tag them.



If your content is solid, they’ll pay attention.



And you don’t really need a big audience. Just share something

that actually helps someone solve a real problem.
