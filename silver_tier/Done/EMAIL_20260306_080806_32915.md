## Summary



Fixes #1906 - Audio jittering/skip sounds at the beginning of words in Twilio realtime example.



## Problem



The Twilio realtime example was experiencing jittering/skip sounds at the beginning of every word. Users reported clear audio with the JS SDK but choppy audio with the Python SDK.



## Root Cause



Small audio chunks from OpenAI were being sent to Twilio immediately without buffering, causing choppy playback. While incoming audio (Twilio → OpenAI) was already buffered, outgoing audio (OpenAI → Twilio) was not, creating an asymmetry that caused Twilio&#39;s media stream to struggle.



## Solution



Implemented symmetrical audio buffering for outgoing audio:

- Added outgoing audio buffer to accumulate chunks from OpenAI

- Buffer audio until reaching 50ms worth of data (400 bytes at 8kHz) before sending to Twilio

- Flush remaining audio on `audio_end` and `audio_interrupted` events

- Updated periodic flush loop to handle both incoming and outgoing buffers



## Technical Details



- **Buffer Size**: 50ms chunks (matches incoming buffer size)

- **Sample Rate**: 8kHz for g711_ulaw format (Twilio standard)

- **Flush Strategy**: Send when buffer ≥400 bytes OR every 100ms timeout
