Here is the truth.



You're not being evaluated, you're being filtered.



The hiring pipeline is built to survive volume, not to understand

people.



Your resume is the input, the ATS is the filter, and the humans

at the end are overloaded.



When a role gets hundreds of applications, most of their time

isn't spent finding the best person.



It's spent saying no quickly.



That's the only way the process keeps moving.



So you get the usual advice about beating the ATS and sounding

polished.



But companies keep upgrading the other side too, more screening,

more ranking, more tools to shrink the pile.



That's why you can do everything "right" and still vanish.



Not because you can't do the work, but because the system is

doing what it was designed ...
