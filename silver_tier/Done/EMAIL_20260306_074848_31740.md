Happy Thursday! Welcome to another edition of Mosh Bytes. Today, I want to share a story from one of my earliest projects that taught me some invaluable lessons and helped shape my career.



Back when I was just starting out, I took on a project to build a website for a book publishing

company where my brother worked. I dove in headfirst, creating the whole website from scratch. But here's the thing-I never stopped learning. I kept reading and exploring, and soon enough, I started spotting issues in my original design and code.



Determined to get better, I decided to rebuild the entire website. The CEO never knew, so I didn't get paid for the rewrite, but the experience I gained was priceless. I tackled the project with newfound confidence, and the challenges that stumped me initially were much easier to overcome the second time around.



But it didn't end there. I kept pushing myself to learn more and ended up rebuilding the website for a third time, again without charging the company. Each rebuild was a stepping stone, helping me refine my skills and approach problems with a fresh perspective.



What I learned from this process was invaluable. Many developers, even those with years of experience, often don't get the chance to build an entire application

from start to finish. They're stuck fixing bugs and doing maintenance work. While there's nothing wrong with that, if you want to grow in your career, you need to understand the whole software development lifecycle.
