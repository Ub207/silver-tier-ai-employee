1. Data Science & Machine Learning

----------------------------------



Learning AI/ML means diving into math, reading research papers,

or setting up some massive stack before writing any real code…



That’s exactly how you can waste months without making any real

progress.



You don’t need a PhD or a Kaggle trophy to build something

useful.



What actually matters is understanding the full process…



Where your data comes from, how to clean it, how to turn it into

features, and what to do once your model gives you a result.



Once you walk through that full cycle, you’ll see how it all fits

together, and stop chasing random tutorials.



2. Frontend Development

-----------------------



People like to say frontend is just making things look nice with

HTML, CSS, and a bit of JavaScript.



But if you’ve ever tried to build something real, you know it’s

not that simple.



Frontend is about how the app behaves when people actually use

it.



It’s about what happens when users type, click, scroll, or lose

their connection. Your app needs to handle loading, errors, and

layout shifts while still feeling smooth across screen sizes.



And to build apps that feel solid, you need to understand how the

data moves, how the UI reacts, and how everything flows together.
