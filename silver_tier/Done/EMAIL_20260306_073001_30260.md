Hey there,



AI might be what helps you land a job… or what holds you back

from it.



Here’s what’s happening right now…



* 68% of U.S. workers now use AI to write resumes. (Resume Now,

Jan 2025)

​

* Meanwhile, 33.5% of recruiters say they can spot a resume that

looks “AI-written” in under 20 seconds, and 19.6% admit they’d

reject it immediately. (TopResume, Jun 2025)

​

* In another survey, 78% of job seekers who used ChatGPT

specifically to help with their resumes got an interview, and 59%

were hired. (ResumeBuilder, Feb 2023)

​

* Even more surprising, recruiter Jaylyn Jones shared how her

resume scored just 16% on Jobscan, yet she still got 12

interviews and 1 job offer in three months. (Business Insider,

Jul 2025)



The truth is...



AI can give you more opportunities, but it can also take them

away in seconds...



And those scoring numbers don’t really matter that much...



It's not about whether you use AI… but how you use it.



And here’s exactly how to use AI to land an interview...



1. Use AI to speed up, not to replace you



* Let AI draft the basic structure: formatting, layout, phrasing

ideas.

​

* But don’t copy-paste.

​

* Add your real achievements and numbers:* AI version: “Improved

website performance.”

​

* Your edit: “Optimized database queries, reducing page load time

from 1.8s → 600ms for 10k daily users.”



This is exactly what recruiters want to see: real impact.



2. Make your resume “hard to catch as AI”



* AI resumes often sound polished but generic.

​

* To avoid that, write with a natural tone + short, human-like

sentences.

​

* Sprinkle in “human signals”: personal phrasing, interests, or a

career summary in your own voice.



When a recruiter reads it, they should think: “This feels like a

real person wrote it.”



3. Optimize for ATS – but don’t obsess over the score



* Applicant Tracking Systems only care about formatting and

keywords.

​
