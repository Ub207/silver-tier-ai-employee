Hey there,



You already know making money with coding is easier than ever…



You just need the right place to prove it...



But how many times have you clicked "apply" on LinkedIn and never

heard back...



You know why?



Because the best opportunities don’t just sit around waiting…



They move to the people who show up in the right places, at the

right time.



And today, I’ll show you exactly where to find them…



1. Private Discord & Slack groups



* Opportunities appear inside small, focused communities around

AI, indie hacking, or dev tools.

​

* Founders often drop a message like “Need a quick script by

Friday, who’s available?”.

​

* If you are active and visible, these chances come to you

naturally.

​

* You can land projects worth $500 to $1,200 with no resume and

no interview, only proof that you can deliver.



2. Open source contributions



* Every pull request becomes public proof of your skills.

​

* Even fixing a bug or improving documentation creates

visibility.

​

* Recruiters and maintainers notice consistent contributions.

​

* One of my guys landed an interview right after a maintainer

commented “great contribution” on their PR.



3. Micro-cohort & alumni networks



* Bootcamps, DevLaunch, and Buildspace are not only learning

spaces, they are referral engines.

​

* People who are 6 to 12 months ahead of you still remember how

difficult it was to break in.

​

* And they’re far more willing to pull you in.

​

* A short DM like “Hey, congrats on landing at X, is your team

hiring juniors?” works far better than sending 50 cold

applications.



4. Hackathons & sponsor pipelines



* Hackathons are more than prize money. They are live scouting

grounds.

​

* Sponsors like Stripe, Notion, and OpenAI watch for standout

developers during the event.

​

* Even if you do not win, you still leave with a new portfolio

project.

​

* You also gain teammates and direct introductions to companies
