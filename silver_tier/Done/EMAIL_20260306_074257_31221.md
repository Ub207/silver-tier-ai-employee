Hey there,



Let's say you’ve got a few solid projects, your resume's looking

sharp, and you've sent out tons of applications...



But no one's getting back to you…



There's probably something wrong with your portfolio.



Because after reviewing hundreds of them, I've started noticing

the same mistakes pop up.



They're small, but definitely enough to make recruiters skip your

profile.



Let's go through them one by one.



1. Nothing makes them stop

--------------------------



If your top project is just another todo app or weather

dashboard, they'll scroll right past it.



But don't get me wrong, you don't need to build something

complex…



It just needs to solve a real problem or show real context.



Here are some examples:



* A bot that filters GitHub issues and sends a daily Slack

report.

​

* A price tracker that alerts you when an item drops.

​

* A resume grader that compares your CV to a job post and gives

feedback.



All of these are simple ideas, but they make people stop and

look.



Because they feel useful, like something someone would actually

use.



2. No one knows what it is

--------------------------



If the name is vague and the summary's missing, no one’s clicking

your project.



Because they don't read your code…



They only skim the title and first line, then decide in seconds

if it's worth their time.



That means “CoolApp2024” or “React Dashboard” gets ignored right

away.



So instead, write something like:



"Flappy Bird AI – A Python project that trains a bird to play

using evolutionary algorithms."



It shows what it is, what tech you used, and why it's

interesting.



If your title and summary can't do that, your project might as

well not exist.

React clone from last year, that's kinda confusing.
