Hey there,



Everyone’s racing to ship AI features - agents, RAG, MCP servers,

tool-calling. Behind the hype is a quieter truth: most teams are

taking on invisible risk. Secrets leak through tools and logs.

LLM outputs slip past validation. Dependencies and containers

pull in vulns you never reviewed. If you’re feeling that tension

between speed and safety, you’re not alone.



What This Means for Developers

------------------------------



Hiring managers aren’t looking for “AI familiarity.” They’re

looking for people who can design AI-native systems with

guardrails, not just prompts. That means you can:



* Lock down inputs/outputs with schemas and strict size/content

limits

* Validate and sanitize tool responses before they re-enter

prompts or reach data stores

* Bake security into IDE/PR/CI so issues are found where they’re

cheapest to fix

* Keep your supply chain (deps, containers, IaC) continuously

scanned and remediated
