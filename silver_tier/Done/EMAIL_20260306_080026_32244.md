Hey there,



If you’re studying computer science or planning to, you need to

read this…



Because most students get this degree all wrong...



They believe that keeping a high GPA, finishing every assignment,

and passing all their exams will make them job-ready when they

graduate.



But that’s not how it works...



A CS degree teaches you how computers work, not how to build

products that people actually use.



You’ll study theory, math, and problem solving...



I agree those things matter, but they’re not really the skills

companies hire for.



That’s why so many students graduate with good grades and still

struggle to find a real job.



So here’s a bit of advice I wish someone had told me earlier…



1) Learn outside the classroom



Don’t wait for school to teach you what real companies care

about.



Learn the tools that developers actually use, like Flask, React,

PostgreSQL, or Docker…



Just pick one and build something small that solves a real

problem.



It could be a web app that helps students track their assignments

or a simple script that saves time by automating a boring task.



Make it work from start to finish, then polish it until you’re

proud to show it off.



That one project will teach you more than months of theory and

stand out more than any grade on your transcript.



2) Stop obsessing over GPA



Once you’re already doing well, pushing for an extra few percent

won’t change anything.



Because recruiters don’t really care how well you memorize

formulas…



What they actually care about is how you solve real problems.



No one’s going to ask about your GPA, but they will ask what

you’ve built, how you built it, and what you learned from it.



So instead of chasing perfect grades, use that time to improve

your portfolio, join an open source project, or learn something

that actually makes you more useful.



3) Use your network early



The best chances often come from people you already know.



Talk to your class
