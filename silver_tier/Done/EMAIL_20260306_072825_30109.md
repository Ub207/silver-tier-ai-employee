The 2 AM realization that changed how I write emails forever

 

2 am in London. Finally finished paying my taxes.

 

Clearing out every single email related to this traumatic experience.

 

Then I stopped.

 

There’s an email right there telling me exactly what I needed to hear.

 

It wasn’t a sales email.

It wasn’t from a guru selling me.

 

Just a business owner sharing a story that most of us can relate to. 



Kind of called me out straight up, really.

 

Triggered me, actually.

 

Which sums up exactly what being friends with Kieran in real life feels like.

 

Lol, jokes aside, I read the email.

 

Every word of it.

 

Now, I have over 1000+ unread emails in my inbox. 

Most of them, I already know what they’d say.

 

Others just have bad headlines.

 

But this one? It had a story.

 

No fancy formatting or proven frameworks, just straight-up realistic expertise.

 

This is what most people miss. They write like a salesman, not a storyteller.

 

We’ve been convinced for years that emails should be salesy.

 

I mean, just look at my other inbox filled with offers and ‘limited time discounts’. 



Then it hit me. 

 

I'd been writing emails like a marketer instead of like a person.

 

The best emails aren’t “5 Tips for Better Productivity." No more "The Secret to Success."

 

They’re real tactical ones that don’t rely on basic broad concepts.

 

They talk about failures and personal experiences.

 

And look, every time I do this,

 

My open rates stayed the same. 

But my replies went through the roof.



People started sharing their own stories. Asking for advice. Building real relationships.

 

And when I made offers? They didn't feel like interruptions. They felt like natural next steps.

 

They were waiting for them.

 

Like Simon Sinek recently said…“People don’t buy what you do; they buy why you do it.”

 

Your personal brand isn't built on perfect content. It's built on perfect honesty.
