****What Is Agile?****



Agile is all about being flexible, working together, and keeping customers happy. Instead of following a strict plan, Agile breaks projects into small, manageable chunks called iterations or sprints. Each sprint usually lasts 1-4 weeks and involves planning, executing, and reviewing a piece of the project.



Agile isn't just one thing-it's a mindset that includes different frameworks like Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP).В 



****What Is Scrum?****



Scrum is a popular Agile framework. It provides a structured way to put Agile principles into practice. In Scrum, there are specific roles, meetings, and tools to help manage the process.



Here are the three main roles in Scrum:



* 



**Scrum Master:** Helps the team follow Agile principles and facilitates the Scrum process.



* 



**Product Owner:** Represents the customer and prioritizes the product backlog, which is a list of features and requirements.



* 



**Development Team:**

Works together to build the product increments.



The Scrum process is divided into sprints, which last 1-4 weeks. At the start of each sprint, the team has a Sprint Planning meeting to move tasks from the Product Backlog to the Sprint Backlog. The Product Owner manages the Product Backlog, and the team selects top-priority items for the sprint.
