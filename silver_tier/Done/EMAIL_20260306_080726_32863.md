The Stanford RNA 3D Folding competition showed what's possible when  

Kagglers bring creativity, collaboration and data skills to one of  

biology's toughest puzzles.



Participants worked to predict how RNA folds into the 3D shapes that  

determine its function — a challenge that has stumped researchers for  

decades. Kagglers made progress that could accelerate breakthroughs in  

medicine and biotechnology.



For the first time, automated methods for RNA 3D structure prediction have  

reached human-competitive performance in blind tests. While many teams  

turned to deep learning, the winning solution by G. John Rao took a  

different path — using a handcrafted, highly optimized pipeline that  

revived a classic strategy: template-based modeling.



With over 2,400 submissions, the competition highlighted the power of open  

science. Explore the winning projects to see the wide range of creative  

methods and ideas that emerged.
